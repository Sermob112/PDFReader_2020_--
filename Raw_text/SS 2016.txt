O
n 14 March 2016, AIDA Cruises took delivery of 
the 11th ship in the AIDA fleet from Mitsubishi 
Heavy Industries Ltd. (MHI), Nagasaki, Japan.
AIDAprima is the first of a new generation of 
cruiseships raising environmental protection standards. 
The newbuilds have an extensive filter system for 
reducing emissions, with an exhaust treatment 
technology that filters all three emission types – soot 
particles, nitrogen oxide, and sulphur oxide. 
A revolutionary hull design significantly enhances 
energy efficiency, while an additional first in the cruise 
industry is the use of the Mitsubishi Air Lubrication 
System (MALS). This modern technology enables the 
ship to glide on a cushion of air, thus further reducing 
fuel consumption. The vessel’s state-of-the-art pod 
drives also significantly lower fuel consumption.
In addition, the new generation AIDA ships are the 
world's first cruiseships to feature dual-fuel engines, 
which, depending on availability at port, can also be 
operated with virtually zero-emission liquefied 
natural gas. 
Ultramodern resource-saving technologies are also 
used in the hotel and restaurant area, i.e., from light 
management and using heat recovery to operate air 
conditioning systems, to laundry facilities with highly 
efficient tunnel washers that use even less electricity and 
water than cutting-edge household washing machines.

BC BIRTE H is the first vessel of a new generation of 
multi-purpose vessels for Krey Schiffahrts designed by 
SDARI. The vessel was built by Jiangsu Hongqiang Marine 
Heavy Industry and delivered in May 2016. The second vessel 
of the series was built by Huanghai Shipbuilding and delivered 
to Nordic Hamburg Shipping in July 2016. Another 15 
vessels in the series are under construction for the above two 
owners and for German Briese Schiffahrts and Zeaborn.
The new ECO multi-purpose vessel is designed with 
future market requirements in mind, notably energy 
efficiency, flexibility and sustainability. To cut consumption 
of fuel oil and emissions of harmful exhaust gases, the ship 
has the GL class notation Environmental Passport EP-D, 
including the IHM, EEDI (phase 3) and a ballast water 
treatment plant. Heavy-lift cargo cranes, the cargo hold 
and weather deck area will be used to carry long objects, 
heavy cargoes, containers, dry bulk and grain cargoes, as 
well as dangerous goods. The two stroke electronic 
controlled fuel injection G type main engine, tunnel 
gearbox, shaft generator and fixed pitch propeller comprise 
the main propulsion system to ensure outstanding fuel 
consumption performance.
The vessel’s hull lines have been optimised using advanced 
CFD and verified by model tank tests at FORCE. To 
improve the performance of fuel oil consumption under 
service conditions, the hull line optimisation was based on 
the operational profile of the vessel.
The vessel’s cargo holds have been constructed with a 
double skin and a double bottom and side tanks. The cargo 
hold area is divided into two holds. Cargo hold 2 is a large 
box-shaped hold (76.5m x 17.6m) and can carry all kinds of 
heavy goods as well as containers. The surface loads of hatch 
covers are 8tonnes/m
2, and tweendeck hatch covers 4tonnes/
m
2. The inner bottom has a surface load of 18tonnes/m
2,a 
block load of 25tonnes/m
2 and is strengthened for discharge 
by grabs. The tweendeck hatch covers can also be used as 
grain transverse bulkheads in hold 2. 
Two wire luffing heavy lift cargo cranes each of SWL 
250tonne capacity are installed on the port side. Combined 
lifting capacity will be 500tonnes using the lifting beam. No 
stabilising pontoon is needed as the lifting operation will be 
carried out using an anti-heeling ballast system.
The propulsion plant consists of a MAN B&W two-
stroke marine diesel engine type 5G45. Rating power is 
4,800kW at 94rpm, complying with IMO Tier II exhaust 
gas emission requirements. To decrease the fuel oil 
consumption, a shaft generator with tunnel gearbox and a 
fixed pitch propeller was fitted.

RITISH NIMBUS is the second in a series of five 
product/chemical tankers for owner ICBC 
Leasing and operator BP that was delivered in 
December 2016 from Hyundai Mipo Dockyard 
(HMD). The vessel was designed by HMD and 
registered under the Isle of Man flag with LR class. 
Unlike the first vessel in series, British Nimbus and the 
third vessel in series have scrubber systems fitted. 
The vessel is an ocean going product/chemical 
tanker for worldwide service with bulbous bow, 
transom stern, flush deck with forecastle, open 
water type stern frame, single semi-balanced 
rudder and single screw propeller driven by a slow 
speed diesel engine.
There are six pairs of cargo oil tanks, a pair of 
slop tanks with one residual oil tank and six pairs 
of water ballast tanks. There is also a fore peak 
tank with two access trunks from the upper deck 
and an aft peak tank.
Four heavy fuel oil storage tanks are arranged 
between the cargo space and engine room and in 
the engine room with a double hull structure. Two 
of those are used for marine gas oil storage. 
Technical fresh water tanks are arranged 
between the side shell and heavy fuel oil storage 
tanks. The strengthened hull, shaft and propeller 
are designed according to ice class 1B. 
An IMO-approved type ballast water treatment 
system based on electro chlorination is installed. 
One exhaust gas scrubber system is installed for 
SOx reduction.

TX Offshore & Shipbuilding constructed the 
110,000dwt crude/dirty product oil tanker 
British Respect at Jinhae Shipyard. The vessel is 
249.85m in length, 44m wide and 21.30m high. Its 
service speed at design draught is 14.5knots at 75% 
MCR power of the main engine without sea margin, 
and the maximum deadweight is 109,584 metric 
tonnes on the summer draught of 15m.
The cargo area has three longitudinal bulkheads 
with double bottom and double hull, and consists of 
six pairs of cargo oil tanks, one pair of slop tanks, 
and seven pairs of segregated water ballast tanks. 
The aft body with transom stern is used for the 
steering gear compartment, distilled water tanks 
and aft peak tank.
A mewis duct has been installed on the ship with 
an expected power saving of about 3% compared 
with vessels of a similar size without a mewis duct.
The propulsion machinery consists of one two-
stroke STX MAN 6G60ME-C9.2 engine, and the 
electric generating plant consists of three sets of 
main diesel generator engines and one emergency 
diesel generator engine.

reducing CO2 emissions by 25% compared with more 
conventional designs using advanced hull design and 
energy saving technology. 
A NOx suppression device has been installed on the 
main engine, which reduces both CO2 and NOx 
emissions by the combination of an exhaust gas 
recirculation system and water emulsion fuel designed by 
Kawasaki Heavy Industries. The SOx reduction method 
installed – a SOx scrubber – was produced by Mitsubishi 
Heavy Industries and Mitsubishi Kakoki Kaisha. 
In addition to the above energy saving equipment, 
LED lighting has been installed through most of the 
ship, with the LED lights on car decks supplied by a 
solar power system manufactured by Solar Frontier. 
With a maximum capacity of 7,500 passenger cars the 
ship can also carry other types of cargo. The design of 
the Drive Green Highway aims to reduce wind resistance 
and provides extra stability. Other sustainable 
technologies contributing to the reduction of fuel 
consumption are the use of a special coating which 
reduces the ship’s resistance and the design of the ship’s 
screw, which increases efficiency. 
Drive Green Highway integrates among the world’s 
most advanced environmentally friendly and energy-
saving technologies, including greenhouse gas 
suppression systems fitted to Solar Frontier’s CIS solar 
panels on its decks. The vessel has one of the largest 
solar energy systems on any ship in the world, with 
more than 900 of Solar Frontier’s CIS solar panels 
installed on the vessel’s top deck offering a 150 kilowatt-
peak of electricity generating capacity. The electricity 
generated by these solar panels will be used to power all 
LED lighting on the vehicle decks.
“K” Line selected Solar Frontier’s CIS modules to 
install on their new ship because they generate higher 
electricity yield (kilowatt-hours per kilowatt-peak) than 
crystalline silicon solar panels in real-world conditions. 
Their strength in hot environments and salt-mist 
environments, such as at sea, will also support Drive 
Green Highway as it carries cargo around the world.

EVA VG is a 5,000dwt general cargo carrier, the 
first of the VG EcoCoaster-series. VG 
EcoCoaster is a new ship concept developed by 
Finnish Meriaura Group in cooperation with 
Foreship Ltd. and Aker Arctic Technology Inc.
The basis of the EcoCoaster design has been to 
minimise environmental pressure, with various 
environmentally friendly solutions adapted into the 
shipbuilding process. The vessels are optimised for 
open seas when operating on relatively low speed, while 
at the same time decreasing the daily fuel consumption 
by half compared with a conventional vessel of similar 
type. This is accomplished by designing the optimal 
hull shape for low speed and optimising the main 
engine solely for open sea operations. Requisite ice 
power is gained from auxiliary engines for the propeller 
through the PTI system/setup. 
Another significant feature is the possibility of using 
biofuel for the main engine. The main engine is the 
1,650kW ABC (Anglo Belgian Corporation), specially 
equipped for biofuel adapted systems. In addition, there 
are two separate independent systems for fuel storage 
and handling, one for biofuel and one for diesel. The 
main engine has a catalytic converter which ensures 
very low levels of NOx emissions. When run on biofuel, 
the vessel emits close to zero emissions of carbon, 
sulphur, and nitrogen. The waste-based biofuel for the 
vessels is produced in VG-Shipping’s (part of Meriaura 
group) own refinery.
The vessel’s environmental impact is also reduced by 
heat recovery from the central heating, air 
conditioning, and cargo hold washing systems, and a 
variety of other measures. The ship is fitted with a 
ballast water treatment system, energy efficient LEDs 
for lighting, and the hull is painted with 
environmentally friendly, non-toxic Ecospeed paint 
that improves energy efficiency.

A MANCHA KNUTSEN is a 176,300m
3 Class LNG 
carrier that was delivered from HHI to Knutsen OAS 
Shipping. The vessel’s design is intended to maximise 
efficiency by reducing fuel consumption and emissions. In 
line with the specification, fuel-efficient ME-GI engines 
driven by diesel/gas injection were fitted with a fuel gas 
supply system to supply NBOG up to 300bar and with 
re-liquefaction plant to control excess NBOG on low 
speed voyages.
The main engine was selected to target the lowest fuel 
consumption with a shaft generator for power take off and 
shaft clutch for free-rotating fitted as energy-saving measures. 
In addition, the vessel is fitted with a propeller boss cap fin 
(Hi-Fin) and a flow-adapted twisted rudder with bulb 
named Hi-Rudder developed by HHI, which together  
represent one of the most effective energy saving devices for 
vessel fuel saving.
It is propelled by two Hyundai-MAN B&W 7G70ME-
C9.2-GI engines driven by diesel/gas injection with MCR of 
2 x 18,827kW. This enables a service speed of 19.5knots at 
design draught when running at about 60% of MCR with a 
21% sea margin, burning less fuel (around 92.5tonnes per 
day in diesel mode).
The vessel has four GTT MARK III type cargo tanks with 
double bottom and double side structure. To handle cargo 
the vessel is fitted with eight cargo pumps that have 
2,100m
3/h capacity each and four spray pumps that have 
50m
3/h capacity each and are of the electric motor driven 
submerged type. The vessel also has four pairs of water ballast 
tanks, two forward water ballast tanks, two engine room 
water ballast tanks and an aft peak tank.

tanks may be used as methanol fuel storage tanks.

pair of slop tanks and six pairs of water ballast tanks;

heavy fuel oil storage tanks; and there is a N2 generator 
room, aft peak tank, steering gear room, fresh water

introducing technologies that reduce shipping’s 
environmental impact.

ORD ISABELLA is an ECO type 2,500TEU 
feeder vessel delivered to German owner Reederei 
Nord in January 2016. This series was developed and 
designed by SDARI, constructed by Guangzhou 
Wenchong Shipyard (GWS) and registered under 
DNV GL class. Based on favourable slot cost 
performance, the first vessel of this series was sold to 
industry leader Maersk Line after delivery.
Nord Isabella’s operational profile boasts attractive and 
comprehensive fuel efficiency due to simulation of the 
ship’s whole lifecycle and a special focus on lower draught 
and lower speeds. The hull form was optimised through 
large-scale CFD calculations based on SDARI’s numerical 
towing tank technology and cooperation with the DNV 
GL Maritime Advisory section, and numerous tank tests 
were carried out at SVA Potsdam Germany.
Energy-saving measures include an asymmetric rudder, 
Costa bulb, divergent propeller cap and highly efficient 
propeller. Trim variation tests were also carried out to 
provide guidance for energy efficiency management when 
in operation and to obtain further energy savings during 
each voyage.
The MAN B&W 6G60ME-C9 main engine is an 
efficient super long stroke engine. It is de-rated and has an 
exhaust gas bypass with load tuning, providing greater fuel 
savings at the ship’s economical operational speeds. 
Moreover, power consumption for the auxiliary engines 
was substantially reduced by the installation of frequency 
controlled sea water pumps, fresh water pumps, engine 
room fans, as well as cargo hold fans that serve the 290FEU 
reefers below deck.
Application of various energy saving technologies brings 
the design into compliance with EEDI IMO Phase III, at 
43% lower than the baseline with greatly reduced CO2 
emissions compared with most similar feeder designs on 
the market.
Nord Isabella has RWO CleanBallast ballast water 
treatment plant installed and has obtained the GL EP-D 
notation. The SOx Scrubber Ready design concept has been 
adopted to allow better flexibility to deal with the challenges 
posed by the forthcoming global sulphur cap in 2020.
After successful delivery of the series built at GWS, 
Reederei Nord ordered three additional vessels of the same 
design from AVIC Weihai Shipyard, adding additional 
reefer plugs to enhance capacity to 750. CMA CGM also 
showed a preference for this design when it ordered three 
vessels at Jinhai Heavy Industry in January 2015, with 
added tailor-made unique features including Finnish-
Swedish 1A ice class, winterisation class notation and a 
hybrid SOx scrubber system.

YK BLUE JAY, an advanced 14,000TEU containership, 
entered the NYK fleet on 22 February 2016 following 
delivery at Kure Shipyard of Japan Marine United. It is the 
first of a series of 15 vessels under construction based on 
data from the operation of the Far East – Europe route. 
The vessel’s hull form allows for improved cargo-loading 
efficiency, mainly through minimising engine-room space, 
while the complete ship design allows for containers to be 
loaded in 18 rows and 11 tiers in the cargo holds and 20 
rows and nine tiers on deck.
The vessel’s main engine is equipped with the world’s first 
system (patent pending) to feature two power ranges (high 
and low). This allows for high speed operations and an 
improved fuel-consumption rate when slow-steaming, 
resulting in a significant reduction of both carbon dioxide 
emissions and fuel consumption.
Further attention has been paid to the efficiency of the 
vessel, with the inclusion of a low-resistance hull form and 
energy saving devices such as JMU’s SURF-BULB (Rudder 
Fin with Bulb) and ALV-Fin (Advanced Low Viscous 
resistance Fin). SURF-BULB and ALV-Fin are registered 
trademarks of Japan Marine United Corporation.
The vessel’s stability and hull strength, achieved in part 
by the structural brittle crack arrest design used 
(developed by JMU and JFE Steel Corporation), allows 
it to operate with minimum ballast water under loading 
conditions.  
According to NYK, these innovations enable the vessel 
to achieve the greatest operational energy savings in the 
shipping industry and cost competitiveness that 
favourably compares with larger vessels capable of 
carrying 20,000TEU.

EASPAN SWIFT is the first of two new dual-
fuelled/hybrid (diesel, LNG and battery) 
ferries to join the Seaspan Ferries fleet. The new 
state of the art vessel was built at Sedef Shipyard 
in Istanbul and is classed by Bureau Veritas.
The 148.9m ferry can accommodate up to 59 
trailers, and the company says that the introduction 
of the new ship will mean a dramatic reduction in 
greenhouse gas emissions compared to current 
alternatives available. 
Seaspan Swift is powered by two Wärtsilä dual-
fuel engines and a Corvus Energy Storage System 
(ESS) made up of 84 advanced lithium polymer 
batteries. Dual-fuelled propulsion, hull form 
efficiency, and a battery backup system are intended 
to reduce both environmental impact and 
operational costs. The hybrid propulsion system 
also permits redundancy while operating at 
economical speeds on a single engine.
In December 2016 Seaspan Ferries, with 
assistance from VARD, FortisBC and Redwise, 
successfully bunkered the vessel using a tanker 
truck to deliver LNG to the ship. This type of 
bunkering operation was the first of its kind in 
North America.
Seaspan Swift and its sister ship Seaspan Reliant

EATRADE ORANGE is a 2,200TEU, specialised reefer 
containership tailor-made for Netherlands-based 
owner Seatrade Groningen. It was delivered in August 
2016. As the well-known expert in the reefer cargo 
transportation field, Seatrade defines this series of vessel as 
‘Colour Class’ with the Fast Direct Dedicated (FDD) 
operation concept. In total, 12 ships (including six 
options) of this series have been ordered, designed by 
SDARI, built by Yangfan Group (Zhoushan Shipyard) and 
registered under LR class.
Seatrade Orange is SDARI’s second generation of 
2,200TEU feeders, with a MAN B&W G-type super long 
stroke engine and an optimised hull form based on an 
operational profile derived from shipping statistical data 
accumulated by the owner. The hull form was developed 
based on the organic integration of SDARI’s empirical 
method and numerical towing tank technology. 
PROMAS, an energy saving device provided by Rolls-
Royce was applied, which further reduced power 
consumption by 2.7% at design draught and design speed. 
The vessel achieved a 32% reduction in its EEDI rating and 
meets with Phase III of IMO regulations.
Another unique feature of this vessel is the special design 
consideration for reefer transportation. There are a total of 
670 reefer plugs with 358 sets on deck and 312 sets in the 
hold. All 40ft containers in the hold can be connected to 
the fresh water reefer container cooling system. Adequate 
space is arranged for each reefer slot to aid daily inspection 
and easy withdrawal of compressors and evaporation fan 
motors. To realise this target, innovative torsional box 
construction was designed with structural strength 
verified by finite element analysis. For the following 2+6 
vessels in this series, reefer capacity was further upgraded 
to 772FEU through the installation of lashing bridges and 
reefer access platforms on deck. Moreover, a nitrogen 
generator system and filter for containers with frequency 
converters were applied for transporting CA containers 
carrying tender and valuable cargoes.
Seatrade Orange also features the application of 
environmentally-friendly green technologies and has 
obtained the LR ECO notation. Technologies include a 
SAM high-voltage AMP shore connection system to 
reduce emissions in port, and the SOx Scrubber Ready 
concept has been adopted in the design to allow more 
flexibility against the challenge resulting from the 
upcoming global sulphur cap in 2020.

ERI CAMELLIA is a 150,200m
3 class LNG carrier 
delivered from HHI to MISC. The vessel’s design is 
intended to reduce waste energy and emissions and features 
the latest energy saving technologies.
It is propelled by one re-heated steam turbine with a MCR 
of 23,910kW. This enables a service speed of 19.44knots at 
design draught when running at 90% MCR with 15% sea 
margin and burns around 144.4tonnes per day. The vessel 
fully complies with IMO Tier III requirements, and the 
main turbine can achieve an additional 13% in specific fuel 
consumption compared with conventional steam turbines.
SERI CAMELLIA has also been fitted with a pre-swirl 
Mewis-duct (developed by BMS) and Hi-Fin (developed by 
HHI), which offer effective fuel savings. 
The design features four spherical LNG tanks using the 
Integrated Hull Structure (IHS) tank cover system. 
Compared with a conventional cargo tank cover, the IHS 
system helps to reduce steel weight and reduces air resistance, 
improving speed performance.
The vessel has been fitted with eight vertical centrifugal 
submerged type cargo pumps that have 1,450m
3/h capacity 
each and are controlled with IAS in the cargo control room. 
It also has nine pairs of water ballast tanks which form the 
double hull structure combined with a double bottom.
SERI CAMELLIA has been built according to the latest 
SOLAS / MARPOL requirements and its design offers 
economical operation, besides being safe and 
environmentally friendly.

generation mid-size, pure car and truck carrier developed 
and designed by SDARI for Shanghai Kingfour Marine Co. 
Ltd. The vessel is the first mid-size PCTC between 2,000 
and 3,000 units delivered by XSI.
The vessel is designed with an air draught less than 24m as 
and folding masts in order to navigate the Yangzi River from 
Shanghai to Wuhu, passing under the Nanjing Bridge. It is 
suitable for carrying various cars, trucks, buses and other 
vehicles such as construction, agriculture, and mining 
machinery on wheels and steel tracks. She has nine car decks 
including one hoistable deck and eight fixed decks, which are 
connected by movable and fixed ramps. Two quarter stern 
ramps are equipped at both sides for loading/unloading 
flexibility. All the ro-ro equipment is supplied by MacGregor.
This series of vessels was designed with the aims of saving 
energy and lowering emissions, protecting the environment, 
improving ship safety, and providing the best possible 
economic performance. The ship’s hull lines have been 
optimised using CFD calculations and verified by model 
tests in CSSRC. To improve the propulsion efficiency, a high 
efficiency fixed pitch propeller and a HVAF (hub vortex 
absorbed fin) behind the propeller were installed. While the 
vessel operates at a service speed of 16knots, the daily fuel oil 
consumption of the main engine decreases to 17.1tonnes/
day. The ship complies with CCS’s Green Ship Rule and has 
been assigned the Green Ship 1 notation. The attained EEDI

propeller design  following analysis by 
computational fluid dynamics.

fired section). In addition, this vessel has carried

Consumption) software, making it possible to sail 
in optimised conditions and enhance fuel efficiency. 
The vessel has six pairs of cargo oil tanks, two slop 
tanks, one residual tank, fore and aft peak tanks, six 
pairs of segregated water ballast tanks, fuel oil tanks 
and fresh water tanks. Cargo tanks are divided by 
plane type transverse and longitudinal bulkheads. 
The water ballast treatment plant is designed to be

recycling, Performance Standards for Protective

IAN ZHEN is a 36,000dwt general cargo ship tailor-
made for Chinese owner COSCO Shipping. It was 
delivered in January 2016. In total, eight ships of this series 
have been ordered, designed by SDARI, constructed by 
Guangzhou Huangpu Shipbuilding and Nantong 
COSCO KHI Ship Engineering respectively, and 
registered under CCS and Class NK. This series is the 
latest general cargo ship type with the largest deadweight 
and largest loadable area in COSCO Shipping’s fleet. In 
June 2016, an additional three ships with Finnish-
Swedish 1A ice class were ordered and will be built in 
Shanghai Shipyard. 
Optimal fuel efficiency is the most significant feature of 
Tian Zhen. The hull form was developed based on the 
organic integration of SDARI’s empirical method and 
numerical towing tank technology. An innovative vertical 
bow ensures better sea-keeping performance and reduces 
speed loss in rough seas. In addition, a flap rudder is fitted 
for better manoeuvrability in restricted waters. Through 
verification by numerous model tests in three basins (HSVA 
of Germany, ASMB of Japan and CSSRC of China), the hull 
form has been optimised to achieve maximum energy 
efficiency over the range of speeds and draughts it is 
predicted to operate at in service. Energy-saving Hub Vortex 
Absorbed Fins (HVAF) have been installed to further 
improve efficiency. The delivered power curve of this vessel 
ranks best within the speed range when compared with 
vessels of similar dimensions and block coefficients in 
HSVA’s databank. 
The main Wärtsilä 6RT-flex50-B engine, with a much 
derated CMCR at L4 point and Delta Tuning method, brings 
greater fuel savings at the economical speeds at which the ship 
often operates. From an ecological perspective, the vessel has 
been designed to improve its environmental footprint 
significantly. Tian Zhen’s EEDI value satisfies Phase III of 
IMO regulations, and a water lubricated shaft bearing system 
is fitted to avoid potential oil leakage. For the following three 
ice-classed ships, stricter EU Ship Recycling Regulations will 
be met.
Another distinct characteristic of Tian Zhen is superior 
cargo adaptability and loading efficiency. Four open-
hatch, totally box-shaped cargo holds with pontoon 
tweendecks at three different levels are suitable for 
carrying vehicles, steel products, bulk cargo, various 
general cargoes and dangerous cargoes. An open weather 
deck provides plenty of space for stowing large size 
project cargoes, and three sets of 100tonne and one set of 
80tonne heavy cranes have been installed on the port 
side. Hydraulic folding hatch covers with fast operation 
velocity have also been fitted.   
In the summer of 2016, as the sister ship of Tian Zhen, 
Tian Xi finished its first round voyage between China and 
Finland through the Arctic Northeast Passage, which 
initiated the woodpulp transportation business for COSCO 
Shipping. Under the aegis of China’s “One Belt, One Road” 
state strategy anwd COSCO Shipping’s expansion plan, this 
series of vessels is expected to play an important role 
traversing  relevant trading routes in the future.
Note: This vessel is the first of its kind to be built by 
Guangzhou Huangpu Shipbuilding Company, but is part of 
a delivery of eight vessels split between Nantong COSCO 
KHI Engineering Co. and Guangzhou Huangpu 
Shipbuilding Company. Information could not be gathered 
on the vessels built first by Nantong COSCO KHI 
Engineering Co. and so Tian Zhen, a sister ship, has been 
selected for publication in their stead.

ORDIS KNUTSEN is a 156,000dwt class shuttle tanker 
that was delivered from HHI to Knutsen NYK Offshore 
Tankers. The vessel has been designed as a DP class 2 shuttle 
tanker and has a bow loading and dynamic positioning system 
suitable for tandem loading operations in Brazilian oil fields.
The vessel is powered by one Hyundai-Wärtsilä 6X72 
electrically controlled engine with a MCR of 16,860kW 
enabling her to sail at a service speed of 14.5knots at design 
draught with 15% sea margin. A Mewis duct, one of the 
most effective energy saving devices, is fitted to improve 
propulsion efficiency.
The vessel’s enhanced dynamic positioning system (DP class 
2) offers greater reliability, operability and maintainability in 
the power, thruster and control systems. She has two bow 
azimuth thrusters, one bow tunnel thruster, one stern azimuth 
thruster and one stern tunnel thruster that are driven by VFD 
motors. The switchboard which provides electricity for the 
thrusters is split into four sections for greater redundancy. A 
controllable pitch propeller and the high lift type of schilling 
rudder are fitted to contribute to the DP operation.
In the case of a single failure of the main propulsion 
system, the vessel is capable of a speed of about seven knots 
and manoeuvring with adequate steering capability by 
using thrusters.
The vessel’s bow loading system has been designed according 
to Statoil requirements on the forecastle deck and has a heli-
deck for a Sikorsky S61.

ALPARAISO 
EXPRESS, 
a 
post-panamax 
containership, has a number of innovative features 
which not only improve the vessel’s operational and 
environmental performance but also provide more 
flexibility during loading. The ship is built to the twin 
islands type design where the separation of superstructure 
and engine room improves stability and provides more 
loading capacity while maintaining visibility.
The ship complies with the IMO’s Cargo Stowage & 
Securing (CSS) Code and has a total of 2,100 sockets – of 
which around 1,250 are above and 850 are below deck – 
making this class of ship particularly well suited to the 
transport of perishable goods like fruit, vegetables, meat or 
pharmaceutical products. Oversized cargo that does not 
fit into a container can be stored below deck. The ship is 
fully equipped for the carriage of dangerous goods and the 
lashing and loading system means there are up to four 
hatch covers – instead of the usual three. This provides 
greater flexibility during loading and unloading. The 
lashing bridges are three-tiered (container layers). 
Valparaíso Express is fitted with a bow thruster of 
around 2,500kW (circa 3,400hp) which facilitates 
manoeuvring in narrow waterways like port basins. The 
main engine was designed by MAN Diesel & Turbo with 
a highly efficient, economical 7-cylinder diesel engine 
with electronic, emission-reducing valve control, 
providing an output of 34,224kW (around 46,530hp). 
There are five auxiliary engines with a total output of 
21,270kW (28,920hp); however, all ships of the Valparaíso 
Express class are equipped for landside shore connection, 
which enables the onboard auxiliary engines to be 
switched off in port.  
The ship’s hull has been optimised for special loading 
cases and speed profiles in South American trade lanes, 
while the ship’s five-blade propeller can increase the ship’s 
speed to 21knots and features an applied rudder bulb for 
energy saving. 
  Valparaíso Express’ range is around 30,000 nautical 
miles (around 55,000km). Tanks hold a total of 10,000m
3 
of fuel, including marine diesel oil for use in Emission 
Control Areas. 
Frequency controlled pumps increase energy savings at 
lower speeds and ballast water treatment units purify the 
ship’s ballast water without the use of chemicals before it is 
pumped into and out of the ballast water tanks using 
filtration and UV light treatment.

184(59). Nitrogen oxides (NO and NO2) are also 
removed from engine exhaust gas by the SCR 
system, in which an aqueous ammonia solution is 
mixed with the exhaust gas and the mixture is fed 
through a reactor.

U PENG is an advanced modern cargo training ship 
for Dalian Marine University China designed by 
SDARI. It was built by COSCO (Dalian) Shipyard Co. Ltd 
China and delivered in October 2016. The vessel is used 
for seafaring education, training and scientific research, 
and simultaneously for carrying bulk cargo, general cargo, 
project cargo and containers. As China’s most advanced 
cargo training ship, it has excellent performance, complete 
functionality, advanced technology, and energy saving 
and environmentally protective features. The vessel will 
play a very important role in educating high-quality 
nautical trainees for Dalian Marine University and sets a 
new milestone for Chinese seafaring education.
As a training ship, the vessel is mainly used for teaching 
and training, including practice for students on the 
management of cargo loading and discharge, as well as for 
scientific research and education on marine science and 
technology, machinery engineering, environmental 
engineering, and traffic information engineering and 
control. Many advanced facilities have been installed 
onboard, such as a training chart room, multi-media 
classroom, network centre, library, sailing simulator and 
radar wave tester.
As a multi-purpose heavy-lift ocean-going vessel, it was 
designed with energy-saving, eco-friendliness and 
flexibility in mind. The ship complies with the Green 
Ship-II and EEDI-II class notations of CCS, and a ballast 
water management system, AMPS, LED lighting, a long-
range fuel oil monitoring system, frequency conversion 
air conditioning and a cooling water pump have been 
installed onboard. 
The large weather deck area is suitable for carrying long 
objects. The vessel’s cargo holds have been constructed 
with a double skin and a double bottom and side tanks. 
Two layers of tweendeck panels of lift-away pontoon type 
are provided in No.2, 3, 4 holds and can be placed on four 
levels, while two layers of tweendeck hatch covers in No.1 
hold are operated by a hydraulic system. There are two 
wire luffing heavy lift cargo cranes each with a SWL of 
240tonnes installed port side, and the combined lifting 
capacity will be 500tonnes using a lifting beam. 
The vessel has advanced communication and 
navigation equipment and an integrated bridge console 
(IBS). The bridge design complies with DNV 
GL’s highest class notation NAUT-AW, while 
engine room automation complies with CCS’s 
unmanned AUT-0.
